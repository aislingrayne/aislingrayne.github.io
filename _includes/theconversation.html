When rehoming wildlife, Indigenous leadership delivers the best results

Moving plants and animals to establish new populations or strengthen existing ones ('conservation translocations') can enhance species recovery and ecosystem resilience. But few projects are led or co-led by Indigenous peoples, or weave Indigenous ways of knowing. In this article for The Conversation, we—as Māori and non-Māori early-career researchers—explain why we need transformative change that brings together diverse knowledge and worldviews to restore ecosystems as well as cultural practices and language.
